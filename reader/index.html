MACHINA

Michael Rock
















Not since  Goldfinger dumped the  gilt corpse of his  double-crossing gal pal in James Bond’s  bed has the metalicized  body had such a  cultural moment. The   intergalactic sheen of the  inorganic seems  omnipresent,  tinging everything  from eyes, lips and nails to gilded temporary tattoos,  cutaneous body  adornments,  to even the most ubiquitous  hand-held device, the rose  gold   iPhone 6.
The comingling of flesh and metal agitates an underlying contemporary neurosis: Where does the body stop and the machine take over?

Perhaps, in the end, authorship is not a very convincing metaphor for the activity we understand as design.
While theories of graphic authorship may change the way work is made, critics must still question what it does and how it does it, not where it comes from.
